A home twin does not start empty, and it does not have to be complete before it becomes useful. This intake assembles the baseline from four places: public records, documents the owner already holds, reports and tools the owner authorizes, and the facts and decisions only the owner can provide. Each item enters with its source, date, and status intact — so the record can distinguish verified fact, dated evidence, estimate, conflict, and unknown instead of flattening them together.

That baseline is what makes the rest possible. It gives every future project a common starting point; surfaces project candidates without turning them into diagnoses; identifies what still needs to be measured, decided, or professionally reviewed; and preserves the evidence later needed for comparable quotes, funding, rebates, claims, warranties, maintenance, and resale. Much of that information already exists — but today it sits scattered across agency records, contractor portals, email attachments, closing folders, and filing cabinets. The intake doesn't invent new facts. It gives each existing source a governed path into one owner-held record, so the same house isn't reconstructed from scratch every time a project begins.

Owner-held has to mean something — so several rules are fixed in the architecture, not promised in the footnotes:
  • You hold the record. Yours to read, export, and carry elsewhere in a portable form that stays useful after you leave. Ownership includes exit.
  • No routine third-party access. A lender, vendor, or outside platform receives information only through an explicit, owner-authorized route, and only for the stated purpose.
  • Pooled sharing is threshold-gated. Shared evidence is aggregated and de-identified above a defined minimum cohort. When a local sample is too thin to reduce re-identification risk, it isn't released.
  • Missing remains unknown. Within the platform, an unfinished field is recorded as unknown — not converted into an adverse inference.
This is the prequel. It shows how the record is built — before any project moves. Already seen it? Skip ahead to the record in operation.

04

How it gets paid for

source: you choose the rail

A project can be researched, captured, configured, and quoted before a funding rail is chosen. It doesn't move into purchase or fulfillment until an authorized rail is attached to the governed scope. The record carries three rails — from simplest to most novel. You pick per project; the choice stays in your record.

Rail 1 · you fund it

Self-pay & escrow

Savings or cash, held in project escrow and released only when fulfillment closes. No interest, no lender — just funds parked against a defined scope until the work is verified done.

funds held · released on outcome
Rail 2 · familiar credit

Cards, HELOC & equity

The rails you already know — credit cards, home-equity lines, equity loans. The record links the account and attaches the draw to a specific project, so borrowed money is tied to a scope, not a vague balance.

linked account · scope-tagged draw
New class
Rail 3 · the innovation

Packet-backed financing

A governed work packet — fixed scope, comparable price, escrowed funds, recorded outcome — is a more bounded, potentially underwritable project unit. That structure is decision-grade project information. It could support a project-specific lending class informed by the governed scope as well as the borrower.

forward-looking · enabled by the architecture
↳
Why Rail 3 is different: a lender normally sees "homeowner wants $8k for windows — trust us." The twin hands them a bounded packet with a verified scope, a comparable market price, and escrow that closes on a recorded outcome. It reduces some of the project uncertainty a lender would otherwise have to price.
One packet, many money chutes

Financing is the first door the packet opens, not the only one. Every money source is really a documentation regime — each wants different proof of the same project. Because the record already holds those facts, structured and dated, it can emit a tailored proof packet for each chute on demand. One record, assembled once; many exports.

Incentive / rebate · needs proof the spec was met

ENERGY STAR, utility rebates, and efficiency tax credits often depend on documented proof that the installed product hit a defined threshold — the right U-factor for the climate zone, a qualifying rating, a certified product ID. The packet sets that criterion as a gate up front and records the installed product against it, so the evidence path is built in rather than reconstructed at tax time.

emits: qualifying spec · installed product ID · pass/fail gate
→

Claim / subsidy · needs prior condition, cause & restoration

An insurance claim or disaster grant turns on documentation that's usually scrambled together after the fact: what the condition was before, what caused the loss, and that the repair restored it to standard. The append-only record already holds the dated before-state and the scoped repair — so the hardest proof to assemble is the proof the twin was keeping all along.

emits: dated prior state · cause · restoration record

The record produces better-documented requests — it doesn't determine approvals, rates, eligibility, or coverage. Incentive, lending, insurance, and subsidy decisions rest with those programs and their own rules. What the architecture changes is the quality and portability of the proof the homeowner can hand them.

05

What you share, and with whom

source: your permissions, your channels

When a project closes, its outcome can stay private or feed a shared record — on your terms. This isn't a social feed; it's a self-selected linkage to the outside world. You set the permissions, you pick the channels.

Tier 1

Friends & family

Private, opted-in shares from known contacts — specific notes on scope, crew performance, and how it held up after a winter or two.

Tier 2

Geographic proximity

De-identified, threshold-gated outcomes from similar homes within a radius — patterns tied to local crew practices, housing age, and regional product performance.

Tier 3

Broader pattern

Larger trends across product classes, crew models, and installation methods when local data is thin.

Share scope & price

Design rules: sharing is opt-in and revocable, the homeowner controls what is shared, vendors get no unrestricted access, and bad outcomes are not suppressed. Contributions attach to specific scope decisions and time horizons — not emotional reactions at the point of sale.

This supplements review sites rather than replacing them. Angi, Yelp, and Trustpilot organize reputation around the provider's profile — and the signal is still shaped by who pays for placement and which reviews a provider solicits. There, the rating flows outward from the seller. Here, the record flows inward to you, then outward only by your consent.

Specification note

Recommended optimized intake sequence

This page demonstrates the baseline-record concept; it is deliberately partial. The complete intended intake is a progressive sequence that moves from least effort to greatest commitment — using existing information first, preserving where each fact came from, and deferring decisions until they become relevant. The full sequence is set out below, each step marked by maturity: shown rendered on this page, partial illustrated in principle but not as a working choice, and spec defined but not built here.

  1. Create and authorize the home record. Address, parcel match, and the authority under which the record is held (owner, co-owner, authorized representative).partial
  2. Load the public skeleton. Assessor, deed, permit, and jurisdictional records pulled on address entry.shown
  3. Import existing documents. Inspection, appraisal, survey, builder plans, warranties, and prior project records — treated as dated evidence, not as current fact.spec
  4. Connect the measurements a project requires. Exterior report, interior capture, room scan, or equipment portal — homeowner-purchased or authorized.shown
  5. Review evidence quality. Reconcile values as verified, reported, estimated, stale, conflicting, or unknown; retain conflicts rather than resolving them silently.spec
  6. Establish owner rules. Export, external-access posture, and a default-private sharing position, set before any outcome exists.partial
  7. Capture intent and generate candidates. Owner objectives, together with items the evidence surfaces for review, each carrying its provenance.shown
  8. Activate one candidate. Define required capture, professional-review class, applicable proof purposes, and funding rail at the point of promotion.spec
  9. Confirm the baseline. Summarize known facts, unknowns, retained conflicts, imported documents, candidates, and permissions before events are written.spec
  10. Enter the operational matrix. Open the new record at its honest stage, or continue into the seeded worked demonstration.shown

A digital twin is a living digital record of a real thing — kept by whoever owns it, updated as the real thing changes. This is that, for a home: one record the homeowner holds, that every project writes back into, so the house’s history accumulates instead of resetting.
